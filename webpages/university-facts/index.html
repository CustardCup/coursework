The History

The University of Toronto (UofT) was founded in 1827 by royal charter, meaning that it was established and granted by the British Royalty. Originally known as "King's College", it was the first established institute of higher learning in Canada, before changing its name to what we know now in 1850. This name change came with the university's seperation from its previous overseers, the Church of England.

Today the UofT is broken up into 3 campuses across Toronto, Scarborough, and Mississauga. For undergraduate students, the university is divided among 7 colleges and over 15 distinct faculties.

The Students

Across the campuses, UofT has over 80,000 undergraduate students and 20,000 graduate students (including myself). Known as a GPA-killer among Canadian universities, UofT provides a rigorous education with plenty of student opportunities to do research. In fact, UofT is ranked among the top 5 public universities worldwide and the top in Canada!

Best Programs

UofT is well known for its STEM research including Neuroscience, Physics, Engineering, and Computer Science. Many undergraduate students apply for these programs since the research is so good. For graduate studies, two additional faculties worth mentioning are the education institute, Ontario Institute for Studies in Education, and the medical program at the Temerty Faculty of Medicine. Again remember: if you're considering UofT for undergraduate studies be prepared for some killer course in first year, including the legendary (but now retired) MAT137: Calculus with Proofs.
